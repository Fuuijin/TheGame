# Legacy System

The mechanism by which one life ends and the next begins — and how the world remembers.

---

## Succession Options (at death)

### 1. Named Heir
A child or close ally the player has established during their run.
- Inherits a portion of wealth, property, and reputation
- Starts in or near the prior character's last known location
- Carries a reputation modifier (positive or negative) based on prior character's standing
- *Design question: how much do NPCs remember vs. forget over time?*

### 2. Ally / Associate
Someone the player befriended or employed — less inheritance, more situational.
- Inherits role/title if applicable (e.g., guild member, vassal)
- World memory of prior character fades faster

### 3. Random Stranger
Fresh start. No inheritance.
- Player starts as an unknown in the world
- Prior run's marks still exist in the world (ruins of your homestead, faction standing you caused, economic ripples)
- Hardest start, highest rogue-like feel

---

## World Persistence

The world **never resets** between runs. Between sessions:
- Simulation continues at an accelerated or approximated rate
- Economy, ecology, and social systems tick forward
- Player-built structures persist (and decay if unmaintained)
- Faction wars and alliances can resolve while the player is "dead"

---

## Run Marks (what survives a life)

| Mark Type | Persists? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Built structures | Yes, with decay | Homesteads, cleared land, roads |
| Faction standing changes | Yes | Wars started, alliances brokered |
| Economic effects | Partially | Price shifts fade; trade routes persist |
| NPC memory of player | Fades over generations | Legends if impact was large enough |
| Named heirs in world | Yes | Live as NPCs if not chosen as heir |
