Each season's real-world impact on a 13th century simulation

🌱
Spring
"The mud is as dangerous as any sword."
PRIMARY THREAT: FLOODING · SPRING FEVER · STARVING BANDITS · ROAD FAILURE
Ecology & World
  • Snow melt — rivers flood, low roads impassable for weeks
  • Ploughing begins; ground soft, exhausting for oxen and men
  • Bears emerge from dens; wolves still hungry from winter
  • Birthing season — livestock, deer, wild boar
  • Spring fever (dysentery) from thawed filth in water
  • Mud terrain degrades all cart and troop movement
Resources
  • Winter stockpiles at minimum — food scarcity peaks in March–April
  • First foraging: nettles, sorrel, wild garlic, birch sap
  • Planting window: wheat, rye, barley — yield not until autumn
  • Salmon and trout running upstream — river fishing opportunity
  • Sap tapping for resin and adhesives
  • Timber felling suboptimal (green, wet wood)
Economy & Trade
  • Grain prices at yearly peak — peasants desperate
  • Merchant caravans delayed 2–4 weeks by mud
  • Peasants hired for ploughing — temporary labour market
  • Iron tools in high demand as farming restarts
  • Debt taken by poor families to survive until harvest
Population & Threats
  • Starvation deaths possible if winter was severe
  • Bandits at high activity — hungry, desperate outlaws
  • Morale lifts as days lengthen; hope returns
  • Plague risk from contaminated spring water

Easter FeastRiver FloodPloughing ContractsBandit ClearanceDysentery OutbreakSeed Loan Dispute
☀️
Summer
"When men are well-fed, lords make war."
PRIMARY THREAT: DROUGHT · MILITARY LEVY · WILDFIRE · CROP BLIGHT
Ecology & World
  • Long days — maximum travel, trade, and labour window
  • Drought dries rivers; water mills slow or stop
  • Hay cutting season — failure means livestock die in winter
  • Livestock on summer pasture; insects carry disease near water
  • Forest fire risk during dry spells
  • Bogs and marshes dry enough to cross
Resources
  • Berries, cherries, early vegetables available
  • Wool shearing — major cloth trade input
  • Salt harvesting by evaporation at peak efficiency
  • Honey and beeswax harvest mid-summer
  • Charcoal burning ideal (dry, safe conditions)
  • Water sources stressed — wells may run low in drought
Economy & Trade
  • Trade roads at full capacity — merchant peak season
  • Summer fairs — bulk commodity trading events
  • Military campaigns drain towns of men, food, horses
  • Food prices lowest of year — brief window of abundance
  • Wool and cloth prices rise post-shearing
Population & Threats
  • Military levy conscripts 10–20% of young men
  • Malaria and fever near standing water
  • Morale peak — fairs, festivals, warmth
  • Banditry low — people employed, roads patrolled
Events & Quests
Summer FairMilitary LevyDrought CrisisWildfireCrop BlightWool Caravan
🍂
Autumn
"You have until the frost. Then God decides."
PRIMARY THREAT: HARVEST FAILURE · GRANARY RAIDS · TITHE REVOLT · EARLY FROST
Ecology & World
  • Days shorten rapidly; first frosts hit in weeks 3–4
  • All available hands demanded in the harvest fields
  • Animals fattened — best hunting and slaughter season
  • Acorn and beechmast drop — pig pannage season opens
  • Fog and rain increase; road visibility drops
  • Rivers rise again with autumn rains
Resources
  • Grain harvest — entire year's food decided in 3 weeks
  • Root vegetables: turnips, parsnips, beets stockpiled
  • Pig slaughter and salting — pork through winter
  • Mushroom peak — many edible, several deadly
  • Optimal timber felling (dry, dense wood)
  • Salt demand surges for meat preservation
Economy & Trade
  • Post-harvest grain glut briefly drops prices
  • Merchants race to buy stock before winter closes roads
  • Taxation season — lords collect tithe from harvest
  • Preserved goods (salted meat, barrels) highly valued
  • Salt trade becomes critical and strategic
Population & Threats
  • Anxiety high — a poor yield means winter deaths
  • Organised bandit raids on harvest caravans
  • Tithe collection triggers serf unrest and revolt risk
  • Slaughter festivals briefly boost morale
Events & Quests
The HarvestTithe DisputeGranary RaidEarly Frost WarningSlaughter FestivalPeasant Revolt
❄️
Winter
"Cold is not dramatic. It just kills you slowly."
PRIMARY THREAT: STARVATION · HYPOTHERMIA · PLAGUE · ISOLATION
Ecology & World
  • Snow and ice lock roads for weeks — settlements isolated
  • Rivers freeze — new crossing routes open on ice
  • Livestock confined indoors consuming stored fodder
  • Wolves desperate and bold — attack village livestock
  • Shortest days; almost no outdoor productive labour
  • Blizzards can cut entire regions off for days
Resources
  • All stored resources drawn down daily — no replenishment
  • Ice fishing — slow, cold, but sustains small populations
  • Fuel (wood, peat) consumed at 4× summer rate
  • Fodder for livestock runs critically low by February
  • Salt meat and preserved stores are daily survival
  • Iron trade frozen — no new tool imports until spring
Economy & Trade
  • Trade routes dead — roads impassable for weeks
  • Local hoarding drives prices to extreme highs
  • Grain can reach 3–5× normal price if harvest was poor
  • Wealthy exploit scarcity; debt bondage emerges
  • Craft work peaks — indoor repair, weaving, tool-making
Population & Threats
  • Elderly and infants die — natural mortality spike
  • Starvation deaths if harvest yield was insufficient
  • Plague spreads in crowded, cold, filthy quarters
  • Morale at yearly low — desperation, violence, hopelessness

The five simulation layers seasons drive

Ecology & World Growth
The living substrate. Every other system reads from ecology. Translation layer between abstract season state and concrete world data per biome tile.
Flora
  • Each tile has a biomass value (0–100): spring +8/wk, summer +4, autumn −3, winter −12
  • Biomass gates forage yields, animal populations, enemy concealment
  • Deforestation is permanent without active replanting
Fauna
  • Animal populations modelled as integer counts per region
  • Spring births, summer peak, autumn optimal culling, winter die-off
  • Overhunting depletes region for 1–2 seasons; predators follow prey curves
Soil & Agriculture
  • Farm plots have a fertility score modified by rest, manuring, flooding
  • Yield = base × fertility × weather roll. Late frost destroys spring crops
  • Three-field system: two active, one fallow. Breaking it costs fertility
Water
  • Rivers have a float value: spring flood, summer low, autumn rise, winter freeze
  • Mills stop in drought. Fords impassable in flood. Ice opens winter crossings
  • Spring water quality degrades (disease risk from thaw)
Weather Events
  • Each season has a probability table for extreme events
  • Spring: flood 15%, late frost 20%. Summer: drought 18%, blight 8%
  • Autumn: early frost 25%, wet harvest 30%. Winter: blizzard 35%
  • Events chain: drought → blight → famine → revolt
Biome Variation
  • Forest, plains, wetland, upland, coastal each respond differently
  • Coastal: milder winters, fog, fish-dominant resources
  • Upland: harder winters, shorter growing season
  • Wetland: highest disease risk, peat fuel available, poor farming
Base Resources
The economic bedrock. Seasons control availability windows. Resources move — they are never created from nothing.
Grain & Food
  • Planted spring, harvested autumn — 6-month pipeline with no shortcut
  • Annual supply determined at harvest. No second chances
  • Storage degrades 5%/month without proper granary
Livestock
  • Cattle, sheep, pigs each have seasonal cycles
  • Fodder must be stored in summer for winter survival
  • Autumn slaughter reduces mouths to feed; produces salted meat
Timber & Fuel
  • Timber felled in autumn/winter (dry, dense, less sap)
  • Peat cut in summer, dried through autumn
  • Fuel demand spikes 4× in winter; shortage is a survival crisis
Iron & Crafts
  • Mining year-round but slowed by mud and cold
  • Smithing peaks in autumn/winter (indoor work)
  • Charcoal supply gates all iron production
Salt
  • Harvested in summer or mined year-round
  • Autumn demand surges — entire slaughter season depends on it
  • Salt scarcity in autumn = meat rots = winter starvation
Forage & Wild
  • Spring herbs, summer berries, autumn mushrooms — all exclusive windows
  • Missing the window means waiting a full year
  • Over-foraging depletes tile; recovers next season
Economy & Trade
Supply and demand driven entirely by resource availability. Trade routes are physical and season-dependent — no fast travel, no magic markets.
Price Simulation
  • Price = BaseCost × (ExpectedDemand ÷ CurrentStock) × SeasonPressure
  • Clamped 0.5× to 5.0× base. Rolling 4-week demand average prevents spikes
  • Grain peaks Feb–April. Timber peaks Jan–Feb. Wool peaks Oct–Nov
  • Local vs regional prices differ — trade arbitrage is a core mechanic
Trade Routes
  • Routes have passability states: open / muddy / frozen / closed
  • Spring mud: −60% speed. Winter snow: same. Summer: 100% open
  • River routes: fastest in summer; frozen crossings in deep winter
  • Bandits target active caravan routes — especially autumn
Guilds & Factions
  • Farming guild: irrelevant in winter, dominant in spring/autumn
  • Merchant guild: summer peak; lobbies against road closures
  • Church: collects harvest tithe; distributes charity in famine
  • Nobility: extracts all year; wages war in summer
Stockpile Mechanics
  • NPCs maintain stockpile targets based on prior winter depletion
  • Pre-winter hoarding is rational AI behaviour — prices spike in October
  • Player can sell into autumn spike or hold for spring scarcity
  • Granary building required for grain storage beyond 500 units
Population
The human consequence layer. Population grows when the simulation works and collapses when it fails.
Life & Death Cycle
  • Monthly: births, natural deaths, disease deaths, starvation deaths
  • Births peak in spring (9 months after summer conceptions)
  • Winter mortality spike: elderly and infants most vulnerable
  • Starvation threshold: food per capita below 0.6/day for 4+ weeks
Migration
  • Peasants flee famine — they don't die in place if escape is possible
  • Refugee influx to stable regions spikes local food demand
  • Abandoned villages lose tax base; lords respond with force or incentives
Morale & Unrest
  • Morale: 0–100 float per settlement, updated weekly
  • Below 20: passive resistance (lower tax yield, slower labour)
  • Below 10: revolt triggers — granary burning, killing of tax collectors
  • Revolts contagious to neighbouring settlements within 2 weeks
Labour Capacity
  • Spring/summer: all hands needed outdoors
  • Autumn: harvest takes absolute priority for 3–4 weeks
  • Winter: indoor crafts, repair, construction
  • Military levy removes 10–20% of adult males in summer
Threats
No monsters. Every threat is human desperation, animal instinct, or natural disaster — each with a root cause in the simulation that players can address upstream.
Bandits
  • Driven by hunger, unemployment, post-war displacement
  • Spring: desperate starving outlaws. Autumn: organised harvest raiders
  • Root fix: stable economy reduces banditry over seasons
Wolves & Predators
  • Winter: bold and desperate, attack livestock and travellers
  • Spring: bears emerging, protective of cubs
  • Population tracks prey — overhunting prey collapses predator numbers
Disease
  • Spring thaw: waterborne disease from melting filth
  • Summer: malaria near standing water
  • Winter: plague in crowded, cold, hungry, unwashed quarters
  • Root fix: sanitation, food security, uncrowding
Rival Lords & War
  • Wars happen in summer — armies need feeding
  • Passage of armies destroys crops and livestock
  • War disrupts trade routes for 1–3 seasons after
Natural Disasters
  • Flood, drought, blight, fire, blizzard — season-probabilistic
  • Chain into economic crises if stockpiles are thin
  • Mitigated by: dikes, irrigation, firebreaks, granary depth
Revolts
  • End state of: hunger + overtaxation + morale collapse
  • Granary burning destroys following year's seed corn
  • The hardest simulation event to recover from
  • Triggered autumn/winter when food + morale both fail

SeasonSubsystem
UWorldSubsystem · C++ · Server Authority Only
The root of the entire simulation. Lives on the server as a UWorldSubsystem — one instance per world, auto-created by UE5. All other systems subscribe via the SimulationEventBus. Never called directly from gameplay code.

EcologySimulator
UWorldSubsystem · C++ · Weekly Batch Tick
Iterates all WorldTileComponents and applies seasonal growth and decay formulas. Runs on UE5's TaskGraph background thread — tiles are independent, making this embarrassingly parallel. Fauna migration resolved in a second sequential pass.

SimulationEventBus
UGameInstanceSubsystem · Delegate Hub
The connective tissue of the entire simulation. All systems publish events here; all gameplay systems subscribe here. Decouples simulation from game — run headlessly without any gameplay code. Critical for testing and emergent narrative generation.

KEY EVENTS

DESIGN PRINCIPLE

The EventBus is the only cross-system coupling point. SeasonSubsystem does not know EconomySubsystem exists. Every system is independently testable and swappable. For a two-dev team: develop systems in parallel without stepping on each other.
