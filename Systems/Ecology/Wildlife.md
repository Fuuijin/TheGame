# Wildlife

Animal populations as ecological actors — food sources, ecological regulators, and hazards.

---

## Categories (MVP)
- **Prey animals:** Deer, rabbits, boar — food and pelts, population tied to vegetation
- **Predators:** Wolves, bears — regulate prey, threaten livestock and players
- **Livestock:** Managed by villages; affected by predators and disease

## Key Behaviors
- Populations rise and fall based on food availability and predation
- Seasonal migration for some species (V2: full migration paths)
- Overhunting depletes populations (they do not respawn infinitely)
- Predators expand into human settlements when wild prey is scarce

## Integration
- Vegetation → prey population caps
- Seasons → migration, hibernation, birth cycles
- Villages → livestock (predator pressure), hunting economy
- Economy → pelts, meat as trade goods
